Animals

1. Cat

A cat, scientifically known as Felis catus, is a small, domesticated, carnivorous mammal belonging to the family Felidae. Domestic cats are known for their popularity as pets and their ability to purr. They have sharp claws and teeth and are known for their independence and playful nature.

2. Hippopotamus

The hippopotamus, further qualified as the common hippopotamus, Nile hippopotamus and river hippopotamus, is a large semiaquatic mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa

3. Giraffe

The giraffe is a large African hoofed mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth. Traditionally, giraffes have been thought of as one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies

Fruits

1. Apple

Apples are a type of pome fruit that are widely cultivated and consumed globally. They are known for their round shape, variety of colors (red, green, yellow), and diverse flavors. Apples are a good source of nutrients, including fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, offering potential health benefits like improved digestion and heart health.

2. Orange

Oranges are a widely consumed citrus fruit, known for their vibrant color, sweet taste, and high vitamin C content. They are a popular choice in both fresh and juice forms, and are grown in tropical and subtropical regions.

3. Grapes

Grapes are a widely cultivated and consumed fruit, botanically classified as a berry. They are known for their sweet taste and are used in various ways, including eating fresh, making wine, juice, jam, and dried fruits like raisins. Grapes are a source of nutrients and antioxidants, contributing to a variety of health benefits.

Cars

1. Hatchbacks

A car with a rear door that opens upwards, often with a three- or five-door configuration.

2. SUV (Sport Utility Vehicle)

A larger, typically more rugged vehicle with higher ground clearance and often all-wheel drive.

3. Sports Car

A vehicle designed for performance driving, emphasizing speed, agility, and handling.
